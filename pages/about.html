About

I grew up in Northwest Washington state where I attended Western Washington University , majored in physics . Joining Teach for America changed my entire life, including my geographic center. After teaching high school for a year in Arkansas, I moved to South Bend, IN for the QuarkNet Program, and decided to stay at the University of Notre Dame to pursue a Ph.D. in theoretical condensed matter physics.

In 2014 I joined the faculty of Wabash College as a Visiting Assistant Professor of Physics. In 2017 I relocated to the left coast, for a tenure track position at Pacific University. Teaching Classical Mechanics and Electromagnetism to hardworking physics majors is the best job in the world. Please see Teaching to read more about it.

I've been collaborating with members of Theoretical Division at Los Alamos National Laboratory since I was a graduate student. The high desert mountains of New Mexico are one of the many reasons to love Los Alamos.

Hiking is my favorite hobby.
